Image Stitching

By John and Sam

Panoramas created through image stitching done with affine transformations. Because of the nature of the transformations, this method works well when the camera is moved in any direction, or rotated side to side (roll), but not when the photographer rotates the photo angle (yaw) or looks up and down (pitch). Using a perspective model would solve these problems, as it would allow for a homographic transformation of the images. Certain images (like the hallway) were also problematic because they either didn't present enough features, or there was feature ambiguity in multiple areas, which resulted in poor fitting.
